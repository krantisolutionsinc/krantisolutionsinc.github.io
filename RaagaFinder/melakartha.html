Melakartha Raagas – A Mathematical Perspective

What are Melakartha Raagas?

At the heart of the various raagas in Carnatic music, there lies a set of 72 core Raagas that are commonly referred to as Melakartha Raagas. These Raagas form the backbone on which all other raagas are based. Typically, any raaga in Carnatic music can be derived from one of the Melakartha Raagas. Melakartha Raagas possess the following characteristics:

  • They should have all seven basic swaras in their Ascent (Arohanam) and Descent (Avarohanam). The Seven basic swaras are:

  • Shadjamam (SA)

  • Rishabam (RI)

  • Gaandharam (GA)

  • Madhyamam (MA)

  • Panchamam (PA)

  • Daivatham (DA)

  • Nishaadam (NI)

  • In addition to the seven swaras being present, they should be present in the correct order of frequency (i.e. SA-RI-GA-MA-PA-DA-NI in the descent direction and SA-NI-DA-PA-MA-GA-RI in the descent direction.).

  • The variety of each swara should be the same in the ascent and descent direction. For example, Gaandharam has three varieties: Suddha Gaandharam, Saadharana Gaandharam and Anthara Gaandharam. If a Melakartha Raaga has chosen the Saadharana Gaandharam in the Ascent direction, it should also choose Saadharana Gaandharam in the Descent direction.

The remainder of this article will attempt to provide a mathematical perspective on the 72 Melakartha Raagas.

More over, R3 will have a higher frequency than G1. Similarly, D3 will have a higher frequency than N1.

Now, we are ready to get to the magic number of 72. We know that all melakartha raags will have all 7 swaras. Take Madhyamam. Assume that all raagas will have Suddha Madhyamam (M1), for now. Let us try to analyze the other swaras. There is only one Shadjamam (S) and only one Panchamam (P). However, there are 3 Rishabams, 3 Gaandharams, 3 Daivathams and 3 Nishaadams.

Let us look at the combinations of Rishabams and Gaandharams closer. At first glance, there may appear to be 9 possible combinations of Rishabam and Gaandharam (i.e. 3 times 3). They are:

  • R1 and G1

  • R1 and G2

  • R1 and G3

  • R2 and G1

  • R2 and G2

  • R2 and G3

  • R3 and G1

  • R3 and G2

  • R3 and G3

However, the following combinations out of the above nine are invalid:

  • R2 and G1: because R2 and G1 are the same frequency.

  • R3 and G1: because R3 has a higher frequency than G1. Remember, one of the properties of a Melakartha raaga is that the notes have to be in the correct order of frequency.

  • R3 and G2: because R3 and G2 are the same frequency.

So, that leaves us with 6 combinations of Rishabam and Gaandharam. They are:

  • R1 and G1

  • R1 and G2

  • R1 and G3

  • R2 and G2

  • R2 and G3

  • R3 and G3

Similarly, we can determine that there are 6 possible combinations of Daivatham and Nishaadam, namely:

  • D1 and N1

  • D1 and N2

  • D1 and N3

  • D2 and N2

  • D2 and N3

  • D3 and N3

So, assuming Suddha Madhyamam, we get 36 different possible combinations of Rishabam, Gaandharam, Daivatham and Nishaadam (6 times 6).




Now, remove the assumption of Suddha Madhyamam. There are two Madhyamams. So, we multiply the 36 combinations of Rishabam, Gaandharam, Daivatham and Nishaadam by 2 and we get 72. Here is the complete list of Melakartha Raagas:
